The Story Order Synopsis

The Kingdom Hearts series is a complex and intricate story that spans multiple games and platforms. The story is divided into different timelines, with each game building on the events of the previous ones.

Kingdom Hearts Union X:

Mobile Game

Set in a simulated Daybreak Town after the Keyblade War.

It shows the organization of how the Kingdom Hearts universe came to be. There are five factions led by Keyblade Masters, each vying for control of the light that remains. It is a prequel to the main series and introduces the concept of the Keyblade War. Turn based game.

Kingdom Hearts Dark Road:

Mobile Game

Set in a world where one of the characters, named Xehanort, is having an apprenticeship for training to become a Keyblade Master. It shows the events that lead to the Keyblade War and how Xehanort becomes the antagonist of the series. Turn-based game.

Kingdom Hearts Birth by Sleep:

Console Game

The prequel to Kingdom Hearts.

Set ten years before the events of Kingdom Hearts I. It is about three Keyblade wielders named Ventus, Terra, and Aqua. Their quest is about the mission of finding Master Xenahort and fighting off the Unversed. It shows the events that lead to the Keyblade War and how the characters become involved in the series. Fighting-combat style game.

Kingdom Hearts I:

Console Game

Embedded Final Fantasy into the Worlds and Disney. Sora is the main character, with the help of Donald Duck and Goofy mostly, with the occasion of others. He helps with fighting the dark forces made from the Keyblade War. It is the first game in the series and introduces the concept of Keyblades and the battle against the Heartless. Fighting-combat style game.

Kingdom Hearts Chain of Memories:

Console Game

Following Sora and the assistance of his friends going into the Castle Oblivion to fight against Organization XIII(13), which popped up as new antagonists in the series. It is a direct sequel to Kingdom Hearts I and introduces the concept of memory manipulation. Card-based game.

Kingdom Hearts 358/2 Days:

Console Game

Set between the events of Kingdom Hearts I and Kingdom Hearts II.

The perspective is now told from Roxas and the showing of the Organization XIII(13). It shows the friendship between Roxas and Axel, and how Roxas is a Nobody of Sora and how he struggles with his identity. Fighting-combat style game.

Kingdom Hearts II:

Console Game

Set after the events of Chain of Memories, Sora is now back to his original self.

Sora comes back to the protagonist and goes to find his friends while battling Organization XIII(13). It is the sequel to Kingdom Hearts I, and it shows the aftermath of the events of Chain of Memories. It introduces new characters and worlds, and it expands on the lore of the series. Fighting-combat style game.

Kingdom Hearts Re:coded:

Console Game

It is a remake of the mobile game Kingdom Hearts Coded. It is about Sora's adventures in the digital world, where he has to fight against glitches and bugs. It shows the events that lead to the formation of the new Organization XIII(13). Fighting-combat style game.

Kingdom Hearts Dream Drop Distance:

Console Game

Set after the events of Kingdom Hearts II

Sora and Riku are now the protagonists, and they are on a quest to become Keyblade Masters. They have to travel through different worlds and fight against the Dream Eaters. It is a direct sequel to Kingdom Hearts II and introduces new gameplay mechanics, such as the Dream Eaters and Flowmotion. Fighting-combat style game.

Kingdom Hearts HD 2.8 Final Chapter Prologue:

Console Game

It is a compilation of three games: Kingdom Hearts Dream Drop Distance, Kingdom Hearts 0.2 Birth by Sleep - A Fragmentary Passage, and Kingdom Hearts X Back Cover. It shows the events that lead to the final battle against Xehanort. Fighting-combat style game.

Kingdom Hearts III:

Console Game

Sora, Donald, and Goofy are back to fight against Xehanort and his Organization XIII(13). It is the final battle against the forces of darkness, and it shows the conclusion of the series. It introduces new worlds and characters, and it expands on the lore of the series. Fighting-combat style game.

Kingdom Hearts Melody of Memory:

Console Game

It is a rhythm game that follows the events of Kingdom Hearts III. Sora, Donald, and Goofy are on a quest to find Kairi, who has been kidnapped by Xehanort. It is a musical journey through the worlds of Kingdom Hearts. It features a variety of songs from the series and allows players to relive the story through music. Rhythm-based game.

Kingdom Hearts HD 1.5 + 2.5 ReMIX

Console Game

It is a compilation of the first two games in the series, Kingdom Hearts I and Kingdom Hearts II, along with their respective spin-offs. It is a remastered version of the original games with improved graphics and gameplay. It allows players to experience the story of Sora, Donald, and Goofy as they travel through different worlds and fight against the forces of darkness. Fighting-combat style game.
